Omelette

An omelette is a classic morning comfort food known to people all over the world.

In all my years eating breakfast, I've had many omelettes but none comes close in terms of flavor than my very own cheese omelette recipe. In my experience, most omelettes include cheese as the filling. It's not a rule set in stone, but most people enjoy a good cheese. So I always pick a cheese(or cheese!) that include a bit of depth in the flavor, such as smoked cheese, spiced cheese, cheese with herbs, or cheese with fruit! It's up to you really. If you prefer a standard cheddar, who is to tell you that's not good enough! Go ahead and grab your cheese and eggs and meet me in the kitchen.

Now that we have selected our cheese, let's start by beating the eggs in a bowl. If you have a small pan, 2 eggs is probably fine, but if it's medium or large, use 3 eggs. Beat those together and then sprinkle in a little bit of salt. Next, melt a pat of butter in your pan and set it on medium-low heat(My stove uses numbers, so I heat up my eggs on 4, then reduce to 3 once I pour the eggs in). Coat the pan in butter, then pour the beaten eggs into the pan, and finally reduce the heat just slightly. Use a rubber spatula to run along the edge of the omelette as it hardens. As the egg firms up, sprinkle on as much cheese as your heart desires. Let the cheese continue to melt. Then as the eggs become more stiff, you will fold the omelette. Using your spatula, fold one edge of the omelette to the center of the pan. Now in once quick motion, fold the part you just folded in the same direction. Allow it to rest for another minute. Now remove the pan from the heat and place the omelette onto your plate. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1/4 cup cheese
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Crack eggs into a small bowl.
  2. Beat the eggs and add a sprinkle of salt.
  3. Add the butter to the pan, and set on medium-low heat.
  4. Evenly coat the pan with butter, the pour the beaten eggs into the pan.
  5. Reduce heat slightly.(I reduce my stove from 4 to 3)
  6. Add as much cheese as you like sprinkled all over the omelette.
  7. Now that the egg is firming up, run your rubber spatula around the pan under the edge of the omelette.
  8. Using the spatula, fold one edge of the omelette over towards the other edge, but only half way.
  9. Once again, fold the same side of the omelette, this time all the way to the edge.
  10. Remove the pan from the heat and slide your omelette onto a plate and enjoy!
